# Advanced Roadmap

A 12-week deep dive into advanced quantum computing topics.

## Prerequisites

- Completed the [Intermediate Roadmap](intermediate.md) or equivalent
- Strong understanding of quantum algorithms
- Comfortable with linear algebra and complex analysis
- Experience with quantum programming frameworks

## Week 1-2: Quantum Error Correction

### Topics
- [ ] The stabilizer formalism
- [ ] Shor code and Steane code
- [ ] Surface codes
- [ ] Fault-tolerant quantum computation

### Resources
- [Nielsen & Chuang - Chapter 10](https://www.cambridge.org/highereducation/books/quantum-computation-and-quantum-information/01E10196D0A682A6AEFFEA52D53BE9AE)
- [Surface Codes: Towards Practical Large-Scale Quantum Computation](https://arxiv.org/abs/1208.0928)

### Practice
- Implement basic error correction codes
- Simulate error detection and correction
- Analyze threshold requirements

---

## Week 3-4: Quantum Simulation

### Topics
- [ ] Hamiltonian simulation
- [ ] Trotterization
- [ ] Quantum simulation of physics
- [ ] Variational quantum simulation

### Resources
- [Quantum simulation review papers](https://arxiv.org/abs/1308.6253)
- [Qiskit Nature Documentation](https://qiskit-community.github.io/qiskit-nature/)

### Practice
- Simulate spin systems
- Implement Trotter decomposition
- Compare simulation accuracy

## Milestones Checklist

- [ ] Implement a quantum error correction code
- [ ] Build a quantum simulator
- [ ] Create a quantum ML application
- [ ] Run experiments on multiple hardware platforms
- [ ] Contribute to a research paper or preprint
- [ ] Present at a seminar or conference

## Research Project Ideas

1. **Error Correction**: Develop improved decoding algorithms
2. **Algorithms**: Find new applications for known algorithms
3. **Machine Learning**: Explore quantum advantages in specific domains
4. **Hardware**: Optimize algorithms for specific platforms
5. **Applications**: Solve real-world problems with quantum computing

## Community Engagement

At this level, consider:
- Contributing to open-source quantum software
- Mentoring beginners in the community
- Presenting at meetups and conferences
- Writing blog posts or tutorials
- Collaborating on research projects

## Continuing Education

- Follow arXiv quant-ph for latest papers
- Attend quantum computing conferences
- Join research groups or internships
- Pursue graduate studies in quantum computing
